Research Introduction

Abstract

Agriculture industry is one of the most important sources of foreign exchange and employment when considering the Sri Lankan market. Since small crops are an essential part of Sri Lankan cuisine, they play a significant role in ensuring people's food security. Experts in the field contend that productivity is hampered by the ineffective control of numerous illnesses and pests. It is regarded as a problem that requires appropriate response and care, both of which can be provided through ICT technology. Hence, this study proposes a smart system that assists farmers and agriculture professionals in providing farmer education, disease and infestation management, and progression level calculations to aid farmers and agriculture professionals in uplifting the agriculture industry in Sri Lanka. The developed 'AI-based mobile application system for Brinjal Diseases' based on eating patterns of Leaf hopper damage is a clever approach that can identify the illness that poses the greatest threat to Brinjal, specifically leafhopper damage. The proposed system informs the stakeholders, Area Agricultural Research Officer, Seed Certification and Plant Protection Center (SCPPC), Agricultural Service Center, Office of the Register of Pesticides (ROP), Plant Protection Service (PPS), and farmers regarding dispersion and perveance of the brinjal diseases and pest infections. Keywords— Eggplant diseases, Pest control, CNN, Mask RCNN, YOLOV5, Disease dispersion, Crowdsourcing, Image processing

Introduction

It has been determined that the "GoviSumithuroo" smart agricultural solution, which includes automated plant disease detection and categorization tools, is a crucial strategy for encouraging efficient farm management. Convolutional Neural Network (CNN), deep learning model training and AI integration are examples of digital solutions that provide the best answer for plant disease detection. Farmers often miss plant diseases in their early stages and over wide areas, making the old manual monitoring method time-consuming and error-prone. The agricultural industry is one of the most important sources of foreign exchange and employment, with the agricultural sector expected to contribute 7.8 percent of the gross national product by 2021. More over 25% of Sri Lankans work in agriculture. Because they are an essential component of the Sri Lankan diet, minor crops therefore play a significant role in the food security of the population. However, many diseases and pests that are poorly managed hinder production, according to industry experts. In the research conducted on the damage to brinjal cultivation, the area of brinjal cultivated in Sri Lanka is about 9,665 ha, the total production is about 108,856 tons and the average yield is about 11.26 tons ha (AgStat, 2018). As eggplant is susceptible to many pests and diseases. They greatly affect the productivity of brinjal and the main obstacle in disease and pest management is the lack of knowledge on the identification and evaluation of the severity of diseases and pests. Most important when considering brinjal diseases and pests, leaf hoppers cause the most damage to the brinjal crop in Sri Lanka. The most obvious symptom of leaf hopper feeding is hopper burn. Hopper burn is yellowing of the leaf margin. This damage is followed by leaf curling and necrosis. Hopper burn occurs because leaf hoppers feed by sucking sap from leaf veins and clogging the veins with the venom of their saliva. After seeing the hopper burn, the plant is damaged, which reduces the yield or the quality of the brinjal. Many eggplant farmers are not aware of the pest's feeding patterns. In addition, delays in communication between farmers and agricultural experts are another factor that hinders Effectiveness of strategies for managing diseases and pests. In order to replace the current manual procedure, which is time-consuming and ineffective, there is a need to centralize data and manage farmers through an easy-to-use platform. Filling in these gaps, the present study proposes an ICT-based application to learn about farming and capture an image of a plant to detect disease. Furthermore, the system detects that the leaf is infected, and then the system detects the level of disease progression in terms of severity levels. It can be difficult to always predict the disease with high confidence. Users can seek support from the community if the detected disease or pest has a low confidence score. A post about the disease is posted in a Facebook group run by agricultural experts and farmers. Their comments on the post will be filtered. Often the proposed disease will be classified and displayed in the system. For farmers who lack technical expertise, this method offers an easy alternative. Additionally, this solution satisfies the needs of industry experts by providing a platform to farmers to disseminate essential control measures.
