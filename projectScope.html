Literature Survey

As a plantation crop, brinjal is affected by many pests and diseases. Brinjal productivity is highly affected by those and the main constraint in disease and pest management is the lack of knowledge on identification and evaluation of the severity of diseases and pests. When considering the brinjal diseases and pests; Leafhopper damage, Hadda beetle and Red spider mite damage[2] is the most important to consider because leafhopper does the most impairment to the plant in Sri Lanka. The most obvious symptom of leafhopper feeding is hopper burn.Hopper burn is the yellowing of the leaf margin. This damage is followed by leaf curling and necrosis. Hopper burn occurs because leafhoppers feed by sucking the juices out of leaf veins and blocking the veins with a toxin in their saliva. Once hopper-burn is seen the plant has been damaged, which will either reduce yield or the quality of brinjal. Most of the brinjal farmers are not aware of pests eating patterns. In addition to that, delays in communication between farmers and agricultural specialists are another factor that hampers the effectiveness of the disease and pest management programs. Therefore, an efficient management system that provide farmer education while helping farmers to detect and identify diseases can be used to uplift the agriculture industry in Sri Lanka.



Research Gap

Real-time detection of leaves available in a plants and classification of healthy and unhealthy (suspicious) leaflets. Detect possible plant leaves and extract them separately from other objects using Yolov5 object detection algorithm. Classify healthy and unhealthy (suspicious) leaflets from other leaves in the plant. Crop Segmenting (separating the background and cropping the region inside the bounding box) the suspicious leaflets separately from each finger in the plant. (Only the suspicious leaflets are extracted, others are disregarded) then provide those images for further classification to identify the disease.

Identify and classify the (Leafhopper damage, Hadda beetle and Red spider mite damage) using transfer learning based CNN models with high confidence scores. Transfer learning based CNN is used to classify the disease. Various CNN architectures are used to compare and find the best fit architecture. The accuracy of each architecture's result is compared, and the best one is chosen to train the model to identify diseases.  Multi-Class Image Classification techniques are used to classify diseases based on their eating patterns.  The custom image set will be pre-processed to reduce image sample similarities and to avoid model over and underfitting.  Layers of the CNN architecture will be altered (dropped and frozen) according to get a good fit model.

Identify the severity level of infection or stage of the detected disease using Mask R-CNN.  The crop segmented leaf image is further processed to determine the severity level of infection or stage of the detected disease by calculating the ratio of total leaf area to infected portion.  The factors which determine the severity/stage of the disease are analyzed. It can be the portion of the area it has spread, or the color texture changes in the leaf.  The masked area is extracted from the image. Color segmentation is used to group different color pigments present in the image in order to distinguish between healthy and diseased pigments.  The K-means clustering algorithm is used to calculate the percentage of damage by calculating the ratio between the colors (which were clustered using color segmentation).

Manual recognition of diseases using Natural Language Processing (NLP) with the help of Facebook posts.  It can be difficult to predict the disease with high confidence all of the time. Users can seek assistance from the community if the identified disease or pest has a low confidence score using a Facebook post  Users can click on a buttons after the disease is identified so that the disease images along with a question will be posted on Facebook as a post.  Experts, farmers, and other agricultural professionals in the group can leave comments on the post. They can provide feedback on whether or not the disease has been correctly identified.  Using NLP, the most frequently occurring world will be extracted and analyzed to determine whether the prediction is correct or not. If not, the community-suggested disease will be sent to the application.

Research Objectives

Main Objectives
1) Real-time detection of leaves available in a plants and classification of healthy and unhealthy (suspicious) leaflets.

2)Identify and classify the (Leafhopper damage, Hadda beetle and Red spider mite damage) using transfer learning based CNN models with high confidence scores.

3) Identify the severity level of infection or stage of the detected disease using Mask R-CNN.

4) Manual recognition of diseases using Natural Language Processing (NLP) with the help of Facebook posts.



Methodology

This study proposes a mobile application to learn about farming and identify the disease by capturing an image of a plant. All of the leafs are detected separately and classified as healthy or unhealthy (suspicious) using techniques (YOLO). Only the suspicious leafs are extracted using crop segmentation and sent through Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) models to clearly classify and distinguish the diseases using feature extraction. The system will identify and classify the diseases, as well as provide aconfidence score for the detection. Once the leaf is identified to be infected, the images will go through the Mask-R-CNN model to determine the progression level of the disease according to severity levels. Users can seek assistance from the community if the identified disease or pest has a low confidence score. A post about the disease will be published in a Facebook group run by agricultural experts and farmers. Their comments on the post will be filtered. The most frequently suggested disease will be sorted and displayed in the app. This can be achieved using Natural Language Processing (NLP). The results of the ML models can be further validated by checking with the answers of the experts.

OpenCV is a software library for computer vision and machine learning. OpenCV was created to give computer vision applications a common framework and to speed up the incorporation of artificial intelligence into products. VAPECA uses OpenCV and TensorFlow for the image processing component.

OpenWeatherMap is an online service, owned by OpenWeather Ltd, that offers global weather data via API, including real-time weather information, forecasts, nowcasts, and historical weather information for any location. VAPECA uses openWeather API for get future weather details.
